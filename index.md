<h1>Hogenesch Lab</h1>
<p class="hero-subtitle">Circadian Biology &#8226; Systems Biology &#8226; Circadian Medicine</p>
<p class="lede">The Hogenesch Lab studies biological timing across molecular mechanisms, genome-scale transcription, and medicine. The work links core clock architecture to tissue-scale rhythmic programs and to translational questions about when physiology, disease processes, and therapies are most sensitive to time of day.</p>

<section class="section">
<div class="section-head">
<p class="eyebrow">Contributions</p>
<h2>Contributions</h2>
</div>
<div class="landmark-shell">
<div>
<ul class="landmark-list">
<li>Defining the core loop of the mammalian circadian clock through BMAL1, CLOCK, NPAS2, and BMAL2.</li>
<li>Creation of Gene Atlas, one of the first comprehensive maps of gene expression across mammalian tissues.</li>
<li>Defining the mammalian circadian transcriptome with genome-scale temporal profiling.</li>
<li>Creation of the circadian gene expression atlas across mouse organs.</li>
<li>Development of widely used algorithms including JTK_CYCLE, PSEA, and MetaCycle.</li>
<li>Extraction of human circadian structure from unordered transcriptomic data using CYCLOPS.</li>
<li>Creation of CircaDB as a public circadian gene-expression resource.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<section class="section">
<div class="section-head">
<p class="eyebrow">Research Themes</p>
<h2>Directions of research</h2>
</div>
<div class="theme-grid">
<div class="theme-card">
<h3>Molecular Circadian Clock</h3>
<p>Mechanistic work on BMAL1, NPAS2, transcriptional feedback architecture, and clock-controlled gene regulation in mammalian systems.</p>
</div>
<div class="theme-card">
<h3>Circadian Biology</h3>
<p>Rhythmic physiology across organs, tissues, and cell systems, including the architecture and outputs of biological clocks.</p>
</div>
<div class="theme-card">
<h3>Systems Biology</h3>
<p>Genome-scale transcriptional programs, the mammalian circadian transcriptome, and atlas-level studies of time across tissues.</p>
</div>
<div class="theme-card">
<h3>Computational Methods and Resources</h3>
<p>Algorithms and public tools including JTK_CYCLE, PSEA, MetaCycle, CYCLOPS, CYCLOPS2, CircaDB, and Gene Atlas.</p>
</div>
<div class="theme-card">
<h3>Circadian Medicine and Rare Disease</h3>
<p>Human transcriptomics, chronotherapy, and published work linking circadian phenotypes to Human Genetics and rare disease, including Smith-Kingsmore syndrome.</p>
</div>
</div>
</section>

<p class="eyebrow">Featured Resource</p>
<h2>CircaDB</h2>
<p>CircaDB is a public database of mammalian circadian gene expression profiles developed by the Hogenesch Lab in collaboration with Andrew Su. The resource allows researchers to explore rhythmic gene expression across tissues and datasets and has become a widely used tool in the circadian biology community.</p>
